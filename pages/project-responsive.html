Responsive Website!

First website of mine that implements responsiveness


Bit of history:

I'm second year into my study, and we just learned about the existence of css grid and flexbox. Naturally when an "ambition project" came along, I wanted to strengthen the newly acquired knowledge by practicing some more,
and also since having my websites be responsive is something very important for me.


Styling choices and End Goal:

The plan was to create a website that is both suitable for PC mode and Phone mode, with the contents shifting to the viewport's needs. With header, footer, and body that consists of 2 halves.

The idea to make the website, or part of it, into a sort of "stim board" came from a new t-shirt I just got, which has a 3x3 grid with different images, whereas stim boards also include gifs.
The colors were directly picked from the website's showcase of the tee's graphic.
After I realized that the website will need to resize to a phone's viewport, I decided to cut the amount of gifs to just 5, but have them be different formats for more interactivity.


Conclutions and Learned skills:

The website turned out fine, it's definitely nothing special, but it does what it was supposed to and that's enough for me.
It taught me more in-depth about using css grid and flexbox, the relation about parent and child elements, making code separately for different formats using @media in css, and making resizable fonts.


Animated gif's below:


All the 3 gifs with hovers on them were illustrated and animated by me. The frames for the first 2 were made in Krita, and the last was fully animated in Aseprite.
